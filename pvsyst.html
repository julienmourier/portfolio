Case 1: Royalla Solar Farm

This is the most complete study, going through many simulation steps and trying out the different tools and options.

The use case is a 24 MWp (20 MWac) PV plant with fixed orientation, near Canberra.

Royalla - Basic simulation - Step 11/15

Setting up the type of inverter and PV module, the MPPT inputs, and the connection in strings and series. We must pay attention that the operating voltage matches the operating conditions (T°C for Voc), among other things. The Pnom ratio is good, the inverter is neither overvized nor undersized.

Checking the sizing conditions, and the estimated overload loss.

    Royalla - Detailed losses

Royalla - Detailed losses - Step 1/31

The first parameter to change in the detailed losses is the thermal factor. We select "free mounted modules", which has better ventilation than a roof (integrated) system.

Royalla - Detailed losses - Step 2/31

This has immediate implication. With a lower temperature, the voltage will be higher. We expect more power output, which also means the inverter will reach their max. more often. Thus, we see a higher overload loss from inv. power limitation.

Royalla - Detailed losses - Step 3/31

Just with the thermal change, the simulation result is much better with 4487 MWh/year compare to 4335 earlier.

Royalla - Detailed losses - Step 4/31

We see that the system is often at maximum capacity. This is trade-off, we can opt for bigger inverters (more expensive) or accept some loss.

Royalla - Detailed losses - Step 5/31

Meanwhile, with the correct thermal factor, the array no longer reaches the min. voltage.
