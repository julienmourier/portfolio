Portfolio WAsP

Case 1: Sprogø Island

Importing 5 years of wind data time-series into the WAsP Climate Analyst, to create an Observed Wind Climate (OWC).

In the next video, we import in WAsP the OWC, and vector maps of the elevation and roughness.

Based on the location of the met mast and its surroundings, we can calculate a Generalised Wind Climate (GWC).

Then, based on the GWC and the topography, we can estimate a Predicted Wind Climate (PWC) for any future wind turbine location.

Finally, based on the power curve of our wind turbine model, we estimate the Annual Energy Production (AEP).

Case 2: Cairns - Step 36/46

We can also get an idea of the complexity of a terrain with the ruggedness index (RIX), which, simply explained, is the amount of steepness. When the RIX is too high (steep slopes), the wind has more of a turbulent flow than a laminar flow, and a linear model (here IBZ) might not be accurate. See Case 7 for a simulation with Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD).
